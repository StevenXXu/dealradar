# DealRadar SaaS - Commercialization Strategy

## 1. Product Positioning (The "Aha" Moment)
*   **The Problem:** VCs, Corporate M&A, and Investment Banks spend thousands of hours (and dollars on junior analysts) manually tracking competitor portfolios, hunting for proprietary deal flow, and guessing when a company will raise its next round. Tools like Pitchbook/Crunchbase are rear-view mirrors (reporting what *has* happened).
*   **The Solution:** DealRadar is a forward-looking predictive CRM. It monitors portfolios, predicts the "Funding Clock" (when the company will run out of cash), and detects strategic signals (hiring, expansion) to trigger outreach *before* a deal is public.

## 2. Target Audience & Pricing Tiers

### Tier 1: The Boutique VC / Family Office ($499 - $999 / month)
*   **Pain Point:** They don't have a team of analysts to scour the web daily.
*   **Feature Set:** 
    * Track up to 50 competitor VC portfolios.
    * Automated enrichment of 1,000 companies.
    * Basic "Funding Clock" alerts.
    * Export to CSV / Integration with HubSpot/Salesforce.

### Tier 2: The Corporate Development / M&A Team ($2,500+ / month)
*   **Pain Point:** They need to track specific micro-sectors (e.g., PropTech) and know the exact moment a target company looks weak (needs cash) or strong (expanding internationally).
*   **Feature Set:**
    * Track unlimited portfolios.
    * Custom "Signal Detection" (e.g., "Alert me when any PropTech company backed by Sequoia mentions 'Australia' on their careers page").
    * Full API access.
    * Dedicated Account Manager.

### Tier 3: The Investment Bank / FA (Enterprise)
*   **Pain Point:** They need leads for Series B/C fundraising mandates or cross-border M&A.
*   **Feature Set:** White-labeled platform, custom data ingestion.

## 3. The Core "Moat" (Why they can't just build it themselves)
*   **Data Structure:** Unifying messy VC websites (some are grids, some are lists, some are PDFs) into a clean, searchable database using advanced scraping and LLMs.
*   **The Predictive Algorithm:** Not just scraping dates, but using AI to analyze the *context* of a company's hiring, news mentions, and sector benchmarks to accurately predict the "Next Raise Date."
*   **The Pre-Vetted "INP Score":** Injecting Steven's/INP's proprietary analysis matrix to score deals automatically.

## 4. MVP Development Path (The 30-Day Launch Plan)
*   **Phase 1: The "Manual Illusion" (Days 1-14):** Build the backend using Make.com, Airtable, Apify, and OpenAI. Do the processing in the background. Build a simple frontend using Softr or Bubble that displays the Airtable data beautifully.
*   **Phase 2: The Data Seed (Days 15-20):** Scrape the top 20 Australian/US VCs (e.g., Blackbird, Square Peg, a16z, Sequoia). Populate the database with 2,000 rich, analyzed company profiles.
*   **Phase 3: The Beta Launch (Days 21-30):** Offer 5 friendly VC contacts or Family Offices a "Free Trial" to validate the UI and the accuracy of the Funding Clock.

## 5. Monetization Beyond SaaS (The "Hidden" Upside)
*   **Proprietary Deal Flow for INP:** As the owner of the platform, you see the aggregate data. You know which sectors are heating up before anyone else.
*   **Advisory/FA Referrals:** If the platform detects a company needing cross-border expansion, INP can step in as the advisor or co-investor. The SaaS is essentially a giant lead-gen machine for INP Capital.
